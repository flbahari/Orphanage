About Rumah Perlindungan Nur Qaseh

Rumah Perlindungan Nur Qaseh is a registered welfare home in Malaysia under Pertubuhan Kebajikan dan Perlindungan Nur Qaseh Malaysia (Registration No: 3255-11-WKL).

Established on 6 April 2011, the home was founded through the initiative of the Melati Welfare Association Committee and its founder, Puan Roslida Binti Che Wail.

Located at Fasa 6, No. 0614, Jalan J 14, Taman Melawati, 53100 Kuala Lumpur, the home provides shelter, care, and guidance for underprivileged children.

Background & History

Rumah Perlindungan Nur Qaseh was established to offer a safe and nurturing environment for children who have lost parental care or come from difficult backgrounds.

Initially, the home was established specifically to support Malay girls from underprivileged backgrounds or those exposed to unhealthy social environments. However, it has since expanded its services to accommodate other children according to their needs. The home provides shelter for orphans, children from poor families, and converts (mualaf), primarily those under the age of 15.

Our Journey

2011

Rumah Perlindungan Nur Qaseh was established to provide shelter and care for underprivileged and at-risk children.

2013

The home began focusing on the welfare and education of orphaned, poor, and converted (mualaf) children.

2018

Educational, moral, and life-skills development programs were strengthened to support holistic growth.

2020

Community outreach programs and public awareness activities were introduced to increase social support.

“Every child deserves love, care, and a place to feel safe.”

Since its founding, the home has focused on holistic development by emphasizing education, moral values, emotional support, and life skills to prepare children for the future.
